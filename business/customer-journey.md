# Customer Journey Map
## Overview
The customer journey map for memory-sweeper is designed to outline the stages a potential customer goes through from initial awareness to expansion of the product. The journey is divided into five phases: Aware, Consider, Try, Adopt, and Expand.

## Phase 1: Aware
### Trigger Event
- **Initial Problem Encounter**: The developer encounters memory-related issues in their large codebase, such as performance bottlenecks or memory leaks.
### Friction Points
- Lack of awareness about memory profiling and optimization tools.
- Difficulty in identifying the root cause of memory issues.
### User Emotions
- Frustration due to decreased application performance.
- Concern about the potential impact on user experience and business reputation.
### Opportunities to Delight
- Educational content (blog posts, webinars) highlighting the importance of memory optimization.
- Free, limited-version memory analysis tools to introduce the product's value.
### Metric
- **Awareness Metric**: Number of unique visitors to the memory-sweeper website or engagement with educational content (e.g., blog posts, social media).

## Phase 2: Consider
### Trigger Event
- **Research Initiation**: The developer starts researching solutions for memory optimization and profiling.
### Friction Points
- Overwhelming number of competing solutions (Datadog, New Relic, Sentry).
- Difficulty in evaluating which tool best fits their specific needs.
### User Emotions
- Confusion due to the variety of options.
- Hope for finding a solution that effectively addresses their memory issues.
### Opportunities to Delight
- Comparative analysis or review content showcasing memory-sweeper's unique features and benefits.
- Free trials or demos allowing potential customers to experience the product firsthand.
### Metric
- **Consideration Metric**: Number of downloads of comparative analysis documents or sign-ups for free trials.

## Phase 3: Try
### Trigger Event
- **Trial Initiation**: The developer decides to try memory-sweeper, either through a free trial or a limited free version.
### Friction Points
- Technical difficulties during the onboarding process.
- Initial results not meeting expectations due to improper configuration or unrealistic expectations.
### User Emotions
- Excitement about the potential for significant improvements.
- Impatience if the tool does not immediately yield desired results.
### Opportunities to Delight
- Prompt and helpful customer support.
- Clear, step-by-step guides and tutorials for optimal tool usage.
### Metric
- **Trial Metric**: Number of trial accounts activated and the percentage of trials converting to paid subscriptions.

## Phase 4: Adopt
### Trigger Event
- **Subscription**: The developer decides to subscribe to memory-sweeper after a successful trial.
### Friction Points
- Integration challenges with existing development workflows.
- Ongoing support needs as the developer becomes more familiar with the tool.
### User Emotions
- Satisfaction with the decision to adopt memory-sweeper.
- Relief that memory issues are being effectively addressed.
### Opportunities to Delight
- Regular software updates with new features and improvements.
- Personalized support and success stories from similar customers.
### Metric
- **Adoption Metric**: Number of paid subscriptions and customer retention rate.

## Phase 5: Expand
### Trigger Event
- **Successful Integration**: Memory-sweeper is successfully integrated into the developer's workflow, and its value is recognized.
### Friction Points
- Limited scalability of the current plan.
- Desire for additional features not currently offered.
### User Emotions
- Loyalty to the brand due to the positive impact on their work.
- Eagerness for more advanced solutions or premium services.
### Opportunities to Delight
- Upsell and cross-sell opportunities for premium features or related products.
- Community building through forums, webinars, or conferences for power users.
### Metric
- **Expansion Metric**: Revenue growth from existing customers through upsells, cross-sells, and referrals.

By understanding and addressing the needs, emotions, and friction points at each phase of the customer journey, memory-sweeper can tailor its marketing, sales, and support strategies to better serve its customers and ultimately drive business success.
